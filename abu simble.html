Abu Simbel is a village located in the Egyptian part of Nubia, about 240 km (150 mi) southwest of Aswan, near the border with Sudan.

In 2012, it had a population of about 2,600 people. It is best known as the site of the Abu Simbel temples, built by King Ramses II.

The Great Temple at Abu Simbel, which took about twenty years to build, was completed around the 24th of the reign of Ramses II.
